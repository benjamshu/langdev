Pronunciation Guide:

Consonants

All consonants follow standard English pronunciation except:

  • h: silent, softens the following vowel
  • hh: makes the hard “h” sound
  • zh: the s in “vision” or “treasure”

Vowels

Vowels are pronounced as follows:

  • u/uh – the u in “under”
  • ä – the a in “almost”
  • ā – the a in “able”
  • ae – the a in “ate” [Note: the mouth and tongue close with ae but remain open with ā]
  • ë – the e in “enter”
  • iy – the i in “item”
  • ee – the ea in “eat”
  • oi – the oi in “ointment”
  • ō – the o in “over”
  • ao – the ou in “ounce”
  • ū – the oo in “oops”
